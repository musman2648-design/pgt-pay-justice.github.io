This Is Not Opinion — It Is the Law

Three official regulatory bodies mandate PGT remuneration. The federal government is in breach of all three.

CPSP — Point No. 6, Notification 09 Jun 2026

College of Physicians & Surgeons Pakistan

"Every trainee must be paid stipend for training at par with that paid by the Federal / Provincial Governments to grade 17 officers. Honorary training is not allowed."

Grade-17 officers receive annual increments every budget. Federal PGTs receive none. This is a direct, ongoing violation of CPSP's mandatory accreditation requirement for all training institutions.

PM&DC — Public Notice, 29 August 2025

Pakistan Medical & Dental Council

"All postgraduate trainings shall be carried out in appropriately remunerated posts and stipendiary positions... Non-compliance shall invite regulatory action, penalties, and potential withdrawal of training recognition by PM&DC under the PM&DC Act, 2022."

PM&DC complaint portal active at complaints.pmdc.pk/admin/login — trainees can file directly against non-compliant institutions.

Punjab Health Foundation — PHF/ADMIN/3758, 16 Jan 2025

Punjab's Binding Formula — Federal Has None

"Governor of the Punjab is further pleased to accord sanction that take home stipend for PGs for 1st year PG Trainees shall be equivalent to 90 percent of take home salary of newly inducted regular Medical Officer..."

Punjab links PGT pay transparently to Medical Officer salaries. Federal government has no such formula, no mechanism, and no timeline — leaving trainees with no legal recourse for revision within institutions.

30 September 2020 — BPS Doctors Get Allowances. PGTs Get Nothing.

Ref: F.No.10-368/2019-H-I, signed by Khalil Ahmed, Deputy Secretary (Budget)

2021–23

Three Budget Cycles — BPS Revised Annually, PGTs Excluded

Federal employees receive annual BPS increments, ad-hoc relief allowances, and other enhancements across budgets 2021-22, 2022-23, and 2023-24. PGTs receive zero notifications, zero increments. Pakistan's 2023 annual inflation exceeds 38% — the worst year since independence.

2024

27 July 2024 — Punjab Acts Again. Federal Does Not.

KEMU issues notification No.9426/KEMU granting 20% Adhoc Relief Allowance on running basic pay to Punjab PGTs w.e.f. 01.07.2024. Stipends revised to Rs.116,708–132,035 (Y1–Y5). Federal PGTs still at Rs.104,390. Gap now Rs.27,645/month (Year 5).

Subject: Complaint Regarding Non-Revision of Postgraduate Trainee Stipends in Federal Healthcare Institutions Since 2020 Despite Inflation, Annual Government Salary Increases, and CPSP Regulations Respected Sir, I respectfully submit this complaint seeking the intervention of the Honorable Federal Ombudsman regarding the prolonged non-revision of stipends for Postgraduate Medical Trainees (FCPS/MS/MD Trainees) working in federal healthcare institutions. I am currently serving as a Postgraduate Trainee in a public sector healthcare institution in Islamabad. However, due to a genuine apprehension of potential administrative repercussions, professional disadvantage, or adverse consequences arising from raising concerns regarding trainee welfare and remuneration, I have intentionally refrained from disclosing the name of my institution. My purpose is not to complain against any individual institution but to seek redress of a systemic issue affecting postgraduate trainees serving across federal healthcare institutions. The matter concerns the continued payment of postgraduate trainee stipends on the basis of a notification issued by the Ministry of National Health Services, Regulations and Coordination dated 27 January 2020, whereby the stipend of postgraduate trainees was fixed at Rs. 104,390 per month. Despite the passage of approximately six years since issuance of that notification, postgraduate trainees continue to receive substantially the same remuneration without any meaningful revision, annual increment, inflation adjustment, or enhancement of allowances. This prolonged stagnation has occurred despite repeated annual increases in salaries and allowances granted to federal government employees through successive federal budgets. During the period from 2020 to 2026, federal employees have benefited from multiple salary revisions, ad-hoc relief allowances, and other financial adjustments aimed at mitigating the effects of inflation and the increasing cost of living. Unfortunately, postgraduate trainees have remained excluded from these revisions despite being full-time healthcare providers working in public institutions and delivering essential services to the population. Background — Who Are Postgraduate Trainees? It is important to emphasize that postgraduate trainees are not ordinary students receiving educational stipends. Before entering postgraduate training, a doctor must complete five years of MBBS education followed by one year of mandatory House Job. Thereafter, most doctors spend one to two additional years serving as Medical Officers before becoming eligible for postgraduate specialization training. Consequently, by the time a doctor enters FCPS, MS, or MD training, he or she has already invested approximately seven to eight years in medical education and clinical service. Following this, postgraduate training itself extends over four to five years depending upon the specialty. Throughout this period, postgraduate trainees are actively involved in patient care and constitute an indispensable component of hospital services. They provide services in emergency departments, inpatient wards, intensive care units, operating theatres, labour rooms, and outpatient clinics. They perform night duties, weekend duties, public holiday duties, and prolonged on-call shifts while simultaneously fulfilling rigorous academic and examination requirements imposed by CPSP and other regulatory authorities. Despite increasing experience, expertise, and responsibility with each successive year of training, postgraduate trainees continue to receive virtually the same stipend from the first year of training through the fourth and fifth years. There exists no structured annual increment, seniority-based progression, inflation adjustment, or mechanism for periodic revision. A trainee entering the first year of specialization often receives essentially the same remuneration as a trainee who has completed several years of specialist training and carries significantly greater clinical responsibilities. Financial Hardship — The Human Reality The financial burden associated with this stagnation has become increasingly severe. During postgraduate training many doctors marry, establish households, support dependents, bear accommodation expenses, pay utility bills, manage transportation costs, and assume numerous family responsibilities. Simultaneously, Pakistan has witnessed substantial inflation and unprecedented increases in the prices of fuel, electricity, gas, food, transportation, housing, and other essential commodities. Islamabad remains among the most expensive cities in the country, yet trainee stipends have remained effectively frozen at rates determined in 2020. Regulatory Non-Compliance — CPSP Mandate The situation becomes even more concerning when viewed in light of regulatory requirements issued by the College of Physicians and Surgeons Pakistan (CPSP). Through its notification dated 09 June 2026, CPSP clearly directed under Point No. 6 that: "Every trainee must be paid stipend for training at par with that paid by the Federal / Provincial Governments to Grade-17 officers. Honorary training is not allowed." This requirement reflects the principle that postgraduate trainees should receive remuneration comparable to Grade-17 government officers. However, while Grade-17 officers have repeatedly benefited from salary revisions, relief allowances, and other financial enhancements over the years, postgraduate trainee stipends have remained stagnant. This situation appears inconsistent with both the spirit and intent of the CPSP directive. Furthermore, I respectfully submit that the administration of my institution has repeatedly informed trainees that any revision in postgraduate trainee remuneration is dependent upon revision of stipend structures by the Ministry of National Health Services and federal hospitals operating under its administrative control. Trainees are frequently advised that unless the Ministry revises the stipend structure applicable to federal institutions, corresponding revisions cannot be implemented elsewhere. Consequently, the absence of action at the federal level has effectively resulted in stagnation of postgraduate trainee remuneration across multiple institutions. Comparative Evidence — Punjab vs Federal For comparison, I have attached notifications issued by the Government of Punjab demonstrating periodic revisions of postgraduate trainee remuneration. These notifications show that postgraduate trainees in Punjab have received progressive increases according to year of training and prevailing economic realities: • KEMU Notification No.9426/KEMU (27 July 2024): Punjab PGT stipends revised to Rs.116,708–132,035/month (Year 1–Year 5) following 20% Adhoc Relief Allowance • KEMU Notification No.11428/KEMU (23 October 2025): Punjab PGT stipends further revised to Rs.123,741–140,901/month (Year 1–Year 5) following 10% Adhoc Relief Allowance In contrast, federal postgraduate trainees continue to receive remuneration based substantially upon the notification issued in January 2020. The current monthly gap between a fifth-year federal PGT and a fifth-year Punjab PGT amounts to Rs.36,511 per month. Formal Prayers and Reliefs Sought: In view of the foregoing facts, I respectfully request the Honorable Federal Ombudsman to: 1. Investigate the prolonged non-revision of postgraduate trainee stipends in federal healthcare institutions since 2020. 2. Seek a detailed explanation from the Ministry of National Health Services, Regulations and Coordination regarding the failure to revise postgraduate trainee stipends despite annual salary increases and allowance enhancements granted to federal employees. 3. Examine whether the current stipend structure complies with the requirements and intent of CPSP regulations, particularly Point No. 6 of the notification dated 09 June 2026. 4. Urge the Ministry of National Health Services, CPSP, and PMDC to establish a transparent, uniform, and enforceable framework for periodic revision of postgraduate trainee remuneration. 5. Recommend that postgraduate trainee stipends be revised in accordance with annual government salary increases, inflationary adjustments, and allowances granted to Grade-17 government officers. 6. Direct the relevant authorities to undertake an immediate review and revision of trainee stipends and to address the disparity that currently exists between federal and provincial postgraduate trainee remuneration. 7. Ensure implementation of a permanent mechanism through which postgraduate trainee stipends are reviewed annually so that trainees are not subjected to prolonged financial hardship in future. Postgraduate trainees dedicate more than a decade of education, training, and public service to acquire specialist qualifications and ultimately serve the healthcare needs of the nation. Their contribution to healthcare delivery is indispensable. The continued failure to revise their remuneration despite six years of inflation, repeated salary increases granted to government employees, and explicit CPSP guidance constitutes an administrative inequity warranting urgent intervention. I therefore humbly request the Honorable Federal Ombudsman to intervene in this matter and issue appropriate recommendations and directions to the relevant authorities so that justice may be afforded to postgraduate trainees serving in federal healthcare institutions. COMPLAINT: Federal PGT Stipend Frozen Since 2020
Budget 2026-27: Zero Allocation — 7th Consecutive Year

I am a Postgraduate Medical Trainee (FCPS/MS/MD) at [HOSPITAL], [CITY].

Federal PGT stipends have been frozen at Rs.104,390/month since
M/o NHS,R&C Notification F.No.9-24/2016-E-I dated 27 January 2020.

Budget 2026-27 has been announced with ZERO funds allocated for
any PGT stipend revision — 7 consecutive years of exclusion.

In the same period:
— Punjab PGTs now earn Rs.123,741–140,901/month (Oct 2025)
— AKU residents earn Rs.121,000–155,000/month
— Federal BPS employees received annual increments (35–40%+)
— Pakistan cumulative inflation exceeded 70%
— CPSP mandates Grade-17 parity (non-compliant)
— PM&DC mandates remuneration (PG Regs 2025 — non-compliant)

I request PM Office to direct M/o NHS,R&C to:
1. Immediately revise PGT stipends with retrospective effect from July 2020
2. Disburse arrears from July 2020 to all affected federal PGTs
3. Include PGT stipend revision in an urgent supplementary budget
4. Establish annual review mechanism linked to BPS pay revision

PM&DC Complaint Portal — Third Channel

PM&DC's August 2025 notice explicitly states: "In the event of non-payment or withholding of stipend, students are advised to lodge a formal complaint to the PM&DC." File against non-compliant federal institutions. Non-compliance can result in withdrawal of training recognition.

PM&DC Complaints Portal →
PM&DC PUBLIC NOTICE — 29 AUGUST 2025

"Non-compliance shall invite regulatory action, penalties, and potential withdrawal of training recognition by PM&DC under the PM&DC Act, 2022."

This is a regulatory mandate with real institutional consequences. Federal hospitals risk derecognition if they continue failing to pay at mandated levels.
